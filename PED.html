Card Reader Troubleshooting

Reboot the card reader.

There are three methods to reboot the card reader:

Method 1: 1-5-9 reboot

  1. Press and hold 1-5-9
  2. Enter the password 190711 and press Enter (green circle).
  3. Tap Reboot. Takes between two and three minutes to get to the arches.

Method 2: XO reboot

  1. Press and hold both the X and O buttons.
  2. Release the buttons when the blue lights for the card swipe path light up.

Method 3: Hard reboot

  1. Remove the card reader from its stand.
  2. Press the two tabs on the plastic block, and slide the block out.
  3. Plug the block back into the card reader.

Card Reader Troubleshooting

Reseat the network cable.

  1. Find the multiport cable by tracing the cable back from the card reader. There should be a power cable and a green network cable connected to it.
  2. Reseat the network cable on the multiport cable.
  3. Reseat the network cable on the biscuit box.

NOTE: The network cable should click into place once it's seated in the port. If the clip is broken off the connector, please contact your OTP immediately to have the cable repaired.

Card Reader Troubleshooting

Connect the network cable to a different port on the biscuit box.

  1. Disconnect the cable from the biscuit box.
  2. Move the cable to another port on the biscuit box.
    • Example: If the cable is in FC-01-B, connect it to FC-01-C.
  3. Move the cable connected to the previous port on the patch panel in the office/tech closet to the new one to keep the device connected to the network.

Card Reader Troubleshooting

Swap a working card reader from front counter to drive thru.

  1. Disconnect the card reader from register 1.
    • Take the card reader off of its stand.
    • Disconnect the cable from the card reader by pressing the tabs on the block and pushing it out.
  2. Connect the card reader taken from front counter to the cable in drive thru. Follow the previous steps for disconnecting the malfunctioning card reader.
    3. asdfasdf
  3. When the card reader screen reaches Select App to Run, select DevIPReg9xx.exe and tap the green check mark.
    • NOTE: You only have a few seconds to select the app, so be prepared to move quickly when it shows.
  4. Select Network 2.0 and tap Submit.
  5. Tap Change.
  6. Enter the register number for the device ID.
  7. Verify the IP address and tap Apply Settings.
    • The IP address end in the register number plus 90. For example, the IP address for register 13 should be 10.x.y.103.
  8. Once the IP is applied, tap Exit.
  9. Allow the card reader to come to the Arches.
  10. Pair and open the card reader.
    • Go to Manager → Support → Support
    • The password is test.
    • Tap “Cashless Maint” for registers. Tap "Pair Kiosk" for kiosks and select the affected kiosk.
    • Tap “Pair PED” and type the code shown into the card reader. The code is NOT case-sensitive.
    • Tap “Open PED”
