DMB Troubleshooting

Power cycle both the media player and the display.

Turn off the media player via the method described in the reboot instructions, but do not turn the player back on. Pull the network cable from the media player. Disconnect the power cable from the display. Let the devices sit for approximately five minutes before reconnecting. This allows the devices to completely discharge any stored electricity. After five minutes, reconnect all disconnected cables and turn on the media player.

DMB Troubleshooting

Reboot the media player.

Each enclosure has two media players. The bottom media player should drive the left panel, and the top media player should drive the right panel. Press and hold the power button until the light turns red. If you cannot reach the power button easily, use a small object such as a ballpoint pen to press the power button. Once the light turns red, press the power button again to boot the media player.

DMB Troubleshooting

Put the display on backup content.

Pull the HDMI 1 cable from the affected media player to induce failover mode and display backup content. This is only a temporary workaround, and you should contact your OTP immediately if this is the resolution. There may be other issues in the background preventing primary content from displaying. NOTE: If the left panel of the ODMB is displaying backup content, the integrated COD will NOT display customer orders.

Did this solve the issue?

  • Yes

DMB Troubleshooting

Check for any tripped circuit breakers.

The ODMBs are on their own circuit breaker, and it may be labeled as such. Find the breaker, and verify it has not tripped. If there is no label for the breaker, check all of the breaker panels for any tripped breakers.
