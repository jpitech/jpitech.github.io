Check the network cable connection.

Verify the network cable is connected to the printer, and check the connector for damage.

  1. Disconnect the network cable from the back of the printer.
  2. Inspect the cable for any damage.
  3. Plug the cable back in.

Office Printer Troubleshooting

Check the printer settings.

On the back office server, check the printer settings to verify they are correct.

  1. Double-click Manage Printers.
  2. Verify Printer Type is set to HP_Universal.
  3. Verify Printer Interface is set to Network Port/DLINK.
  4. Make any changes necessary and click Exit.

Remove and shake the toner cartridge.

Pull out the toner cartridge and shake it vigorously to loosen the toner powder in the cartridge. NOTE: If shaking the toner cartridge fixes your issue, the cartridge will need to be replaced in the near future.

Office Printer Troubleshooting

Replace the imaging drum.

NOTE: This only applies if your printer uses a 30A toner cartridge. If your printer uses a 78A toner cartridge, your imaging drum is built into the toner cartridge.

If your printer has a separate imaging drum and you do not have one on hand, you will need to order one with the supply order form. The imaging drum is a 32A cartridge.
