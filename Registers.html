Register Troubleshooting

Check the power cable.

Make sure the power cable is properly seated on both the register and in the twist-lock outlet.

If you have JS-960s in your store:

  • The power cable plugs into the bottom of the register.
  • Tilt the register and push the cable in as far as it will go.

If you have JS-970s in your store:

  • The power cable plugs into the brick behind the stand cover.
  • Tilt the screen until it's flat (parallel to the counter).
  • Reach under the register on the customer side and find two small tabs.
  • Pull those tabs to the outside of the register.
  • Pull the cover off the front of the register.
  • Push the power cable into the power brick as far as it will go.
  • Follow these steps in reverse to attach the cover.

Register Troubleshooting

Reboot the register.

Press the power button on the register once to shut it down. On JS-960 models, the power button may be slightly covered by plastic, so you may need a pen or something similar to press the button. DO NOT press and hold the power button unless there is no other option! This may cause data corruption or damage to the unit!

Once the register shuts down, press the power button again to boot it back up.

Register Troubleshooting

Cycle the cash drawer mode.

  1. Go to the Manager menu.
  2. Select Manager Options.
  3. Change the cash drawer mode from Normal to Forced.
  4. Reboot the register.
  5. Go back to the Manager menu.
  6. Select Manager Options.
  7. Change the cash drawer mode back to Normal.
  8. Test the drawer by performing a manual drawer open or wait for the next cash transaction.
